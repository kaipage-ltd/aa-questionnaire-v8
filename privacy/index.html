Privacy Notice

How we use your diagnostic data.

This notice explains how Atelier + Avenue uses personal data when you complete the AI Readiness Diagnostic and request your profile, reveal page and PDF.

Version 2026-06-25 | Last updated 25 June 2026

Who is responsible

The controller for the AI Readiness Diagnostic is Atelier Consulting B.V., trading as Atelier + Avenue, incorporated and registered in the Netherlands (company registration number 75907429), with registered office at Herengracht 449A, 1017 BR Amsterdam, the Netherlands. For any privacy question or to exercise your rights, contact us at hello@atelierandavenue.com.

What we collect

  • Your name, work email address and, if you provide it, your company name.
  • Your questionnaire answers and the profile, score, weakest area, action plan, reveal link and PDF link generated from those answers.
  • Technical request data needed to run and secure the service, such as request metadata, IP address and operational logs.

How we use it

  • To generate and deliver your AI-readiness profile, reveal page and PDF.
  • To create or update a Brevo contact record with your diagnostic links and profile metadata.
  • To operate, secure, troubleshoot and improve the diagnostic.
  • To follow up about the diagnostic or a working-session request connected to it.

Lawful bases

We rely on legitimate interests to provide the diagnostic you requested, keep the service secure and follow up about the result. If you ask us to discuss A+A services, we may also rely on taking steps at your request before entering a contract. We may rely on legal obligation where we need to keep or disclose information to comply with law.

This acknowledgement is not marketing consent. Marketing emails are handled separately and only sent where consent or another lawful route under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the EU ePrivacy Directive applies.

Who receives it

We use the following processors to run the diagnostic:

  • Vercel hosts and runs the application and its serverless functions.
  • Brevo holds the contact record and, once sender authentication is complete, delivers the requested profile email.
  • Cloudflare provides the Turnstile anti-bot check on the final form. It receives your IP address and limited browser and device signals to confirm the submission is human.

We may also share information with professional advisers or service providers where necessary to run the business or comply with law. We do not sell your personal data.

Cookies and similar technologies

We do not use advertising or analytics cookies on the diagnostic. To protect the final form from automated abuse we load Cloudflare Turnstile, which is strictly necessary to provide the service. Turnstile may store a short-lived token in your browser and read limited device and network signals to tell humans from bots. It is not used for advertising or cross-site tracking.

International transfers

Some of our processors are based outside the EU/EEA. Brevo processes data within the EU. Vercel and Cloudflare are US-based and may process data in the United States. Where personal data leaves the EU/EEA we rely on appropriate safeguards such as a European Commission adequacy decision, EU Standard Contractual Clauses, or the EU-US Data Privacy Framework, together with any additional measures offered by those processors.

Retention

The reveal and PDF links use a sealed token that expires after 14 days. We do not run a separate questionnaire database in this app. Brevo contact and diagnostic metadata are kept for up to 24 months after the last meaningful interaction, unless you ask us to delete them sooner or we need to keep limited records for legal reasons. Operational and security logs held by our hosting and security providers are kept for a limited period, generally no longer than 30 days, after which they are deleted or aggregated.

Your rights

You can ask to access, correct, delete or restrict your personal data, receive a portable copy, object to processing based on legitimate interests, and withdraw marketing consent where consent is used.

You also have the right to complain to a supervisory authority. Our lead authority is the Dutch Data Protection Authority (Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens, autoriteitpersoonsgegevens.nl); you may also complain to the supervisory authority in your own country.

Automated profiling

The diagnostic automatically turns your answers into an indicative business profile and action plan. It does not make decisions about you with legal or similarly significant effects.

If you do not provide the data

Name, work email, questionnaire answers and this privacy acknowledgement are required to generate and deliver the profile. Without them, we cannot provide the reveal or PDF.
